Analyzing the proposed literature stated by the article all three sources introduce the CVSS as a vulnerability assessment tool (Scarfone et al., 2008; Souppaya and Scarfone, 2013; PCI security standards, 2022). Most of the critical claims made by Spring et al. (2021) seem to originate from the interpretation of terminology between vulnerability and risk, which Wolf (2012) compared to the Babylonian confusion.

Therefore to rightfully analyze the article by Spring et al. (2021), it is important to define these terms. Stallings and Brown (2015) distinguish between vulnerabilities, threats, and risks. This article makes a correlation between these three and how they complement and evolve into eventually a risk. As claimed by the CVSS, the calculator should not be used for a risk score.

Network Security - Initial Post (Unit 1)

Digitalisation – What are the security implications of the digital economy?

November 13, 2023 Wiley Finch

Technology is changing the world at a never-before-seen pace. Therefore the need to adapt traditional brick-and-mortar SMEs to the digital world is now more relevant than ever. In a survey by Wei, Sanbron, and Slaughter (2019) in the utilities and power industry four faces of adapting digitalization in an organization are defined. Most companies in this survey get stuck at “doing”-digital but never becoming fully digital. “Being” fully digital is defined as: “Business, operating, and customer models are optimized for digital and profoundly different from prior business, operating and customer models” (Wei, Sanbron, and Slaughter, 2019).

The more traditional bricks-and-mortar SMEs implement digitalization the more their “cyber”-surface broadens, opening up new risks for an organization. On the other hand, this group tends to have the misconception they are safe because their digital assets are not interesting to cyber attackers (Chidukwani, Zander and Koutsakis, 2022). One example might be implementing a BYOD strategy, this step of digitalization requires a next step in controlling cyber threats. Losing a mobile device is one of the most likely threats to happen according to Spremić and Šimunic (2018). One benefit of “late” adaptation of digitalization is knowledge of best practices on putting controls in place to best handle these kinds of incidents.

Launching into CS - Summary Post (Unit 8)

Identification and discussion of two security technologies and the context in which they can be employed.

October 2, 2023 Wiley Finch

Denial of Service (DoS) attacks are designed to attack the availability of the victim’s resources. In case of a possible successful attack, it is one of the most visible ones, since resources are limited to not available resulting in frustrated users, lost business, and a damaged reputation. The Distributed DoS (DDoS) attack has a growing number of reported incidents, making it one of the most important and fatal threats (Mansfield-Devine, 2015). The goal of these attacks is to obstruct the legitimate flow of traffic by flooding the target with traffic. The seriousness of this attack can be found in an example that was provided by Dr. Maria Alvanou: in November 2022 a DDoS attack originating from the Netherlands was responsible for 800 websites of the Greece government being frozen, even blocking electronic prescriptions (Τσιλιόπουλος, 2022). This summary post will focus on two cyber security technologies that help protect against DDoS attacks.

The first proposed technology is to adopt a Demilitarized Zone (DMZ) in the network design in combination with a Next Generation Firewall (NGFW). A DMZ is a carefully controlled perimeter network that is used as a barrier between public or non-trusted networks and internal or protected networks (Rogers, 2022). The performance of a DMZ is more effective if it is employed in combination with one or two firewalls (s) (Rababah, Zhou, and Bader, 2018). Patel (2020) has demonstrated that a network designed with one firewall (Cisco ASA) provided enhanced security and performance against TCP SYN flood and ICMP flood attacks. This, however, as stated in response to the initial response by Abdallah Hamad, only protects against DDoS attacks on the network layer of the OSI model, and thus the use of Web Application Firewalls was proposed. Although this is absolutely true, it is better to enhance the security design of Patel (2020) by switching out the traditional firewall in favor of an NGFW. NGFWs have functionalities that protect against layer 3, 4, and 7 (the application layer) attacks, resulting in the most complete protection in the network design (J. Liang and Y. Kim, 2022). Leaving the designing and employing of this technology the biggest challenge, especially in virtual environments, such as complexity, and risk of misconfiguration (Kasapovic and Biberkic, 2015).

Summary on: Discussion on why Cyber Security is now a global issue and why it is important for companies to invest in Cyber Security.

August 28, 2023 Wiley Finch

The initial post discussed why Cyber Security is a global issue and the importance for organizations to invest in Cyber Security. Since communications go over traditional lines there is a hypothetical possibility and necessity to define cyber borders and secure them (Osborn, 2017). As well noticed by Kemi Fadiya based on the finding of Kennedy (2014): “the internet being global and the cyberspace unregulated, it is the responsibility of governments and companies to secure their networks as the repercussions of a cyber-attack not only affects them but society at large”.

As stated in the original post, this responsibility as described in this quote is mostly taken after an impactful incident (Arroyabe et al., 2023). Reflecting on the example of the initial post, the cyber-attack by Lapsus$ on Uber, it is clear that Uber's investments changed after the incident. Discussion on why Cyber Security is now a global issue and why it is important for companies to invest in Cyber Security.

August 22, 2023 Wiley Finch

The interconnection between devices globally, via for example the internet, creates the popular belief that cyberspace holds no borders. Although not as acknowledged and clear as physical borders, there most definitely is a necessity to define cyber borders and secure them as discussed by Osborn (2017).

Cyberspace philosophically speaking aims to create a more open world and society. This open world means that, although we have the rise of cyber borders, there should be little to no problems to connect with the entire world. Every possible threat to this open cyberspace is considered part of the “cyber threat landscape”. As technology and digitalization are advancing at an alarming pace so does the magnitude of cyberspace and the “cyber threat landscape”.
